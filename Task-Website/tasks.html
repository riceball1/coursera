Avoid using disposable products

Disposable products such as spoons, forks, cups, or napkins often found in restaurants can create a lot of pollution. You can eliminate excess waste by bringing your own utensils, reusable water bottles, and cloth napkins when going out.

Use bar soap instead of liquid soap

Did you know that liquid soap is mostly water? The packing of most liquid soaps is plastic. By eliminating purchasing liquid soap, such as body wash or shampoo, you will be also reducing unncessary transport of plastic and harsh chemicals. Bar soap is easy to turn into liquid soap at home, and most often the packaging is small and biodegradable. You can shave bar soap into a glass container and heat it up with boiling water. Let it cool and you can now use the liquid soap! It's easy as that!

Buy good quality clothes less often

Fast fashion or cheap products is the number 2 polluting industry, with the oil industry being the number 1. You can do more by shopping less for clothes and buying good quality clothes from reliable sources. The idea of buying clothes whenver you like is part of consumptionism and feeds into materialistic tendencies. Eliminating the constant need to shop and buying from sustainable, fair-trade companies can do more to reduce the negative environmental impacts and societal impacts created by cheap fashion and overproduction of material goods.

Use a reusable grocery bag

Plastic is difficult to breakdown and is an unhealthy product that is often used in disposable products. By avoidng plastics and using a reusable spoon, grocery bag, or non-plastic containers for storing food or other goods, you will promote less production of plastics. Therefore ending the unneeded waste put into the landfills and our oceans.

Compost or recycle

Instead of tossing away that cardboard box or glass bottle into the trash can, why not recycle? You can find many cities and businesses these days have some sort of recycling. And if you have your own garden you can compost left over vegetables or fruits into the dirt. This way you eliminate extra greenhouse gases and toxins from getting into our air and land
